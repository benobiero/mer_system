Monitoring and Evaluation

Monitoring refers to the systematic collection and analysis of data on specified indicators to assess the progress and effectiveness of a project or program. It is a continuous process that provides information on the implementation of a project or program, and is used to identify problems or issues in a timely manner, so that corrective action can be taken.

Evaluation, on the other hand, is a systematic and objective assessment of an ongoing or completed project or program. It involves the collection and analysis of data and information to determine the extent to which the objectives of the project or program have been achieved, and to identify the strengths and weaknesses of the project or program. Evaluation is typically conducted at the end of a project or program, but can also be conducted at various points during the life of the project or program.
